Hazrat Khadijah (R.A.) - The Noble Businesswoman

Hazrat Khadijah (R.A.), the first wife of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), was a highly successful and respected businesswoman in pre-Islamic Arabia. Her business acumen and ethical trade practices set her apart in the trading hub of Mecca.

1. Family Background & Inheritance

  • Khadijah (R.A.) belonged to the noble and wealthy Qurayshi tribe, the Banu Asad.
  • She inherited her father's business after his death, as he was also a successful merchant.

2. Trade & Caravan Business

  • She was known as "Tahira" (the Pure One) and "Ameerat-Quraysh" (Princess of Quraysh) due to her honorable reputation.
  • She primarily traded in luxury goods like perfumes, silk, spices, and leather, which were transported via caravans to Syria, Yemen, and other regions.
  • She employed agents to manage trade on her behalf, as women in that era did not travel with caravans.

3. Hiring Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) & Marriage

  • Due to his reputation for honesty (Al-Amin â€“ the Trustworthy), she hired the young Muhammad (PBUH) to lead her trade caravan to Syria.
  • Her slave, Maysarah, accompanied him and reported back on his exceptional integrity and success in trade.
  • Impressed by his character, she proposed marriage through her friend Nafeesah, and they married when he was 25 and she was 40.

4. Business Ethics & Success Factors

  • Trust & Fair Dealings: She was known for honest transactions, avoiding exploitation and usury (interest-based trade).
  • Wise Investments: She reinvested profits wisely, expanding her trade networks.
  • Generosity: She used her wealth to help the poor and support the early Muslim community.

5. Support for Islam

  • After Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) received the first revelation, she was the first to believe in him and used her wealth to support the struggling Muslim community.

Conclusion

Hazrat Khadijah (R.A.) was a visionary businesswoman who combined sharp trade skills with strong ethics, setting an example for Muslim women in commerce. Her success was not just in wealth but in her integrity and support for Islam.
