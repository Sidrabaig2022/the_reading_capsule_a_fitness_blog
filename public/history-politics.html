History & Politics

The Magna Carta, signed in 1215, was the first document to limit the power of the English monarch and laid the foundation for modern democracy.
The French Revolution, beginning in 1789, dramatically changed the course of European history by challenging the absolute power of monarchy and inspiring revolutions around the world.
The Cold War, lasting from 1947 to 1991, was a geopolitical struggle between the United States and the Soviet Union without direct large-scale fighting between the two superpowers.
The Roman Empire once covered more than 5 million square kilometers at its peak and had a profound impact on law, politics, language, and architecture.
Nelson Mandela spent 27 years in prison before becoming South Africa's first Black president in 1994 and a symbol of resistance against apartheid.
The American Declaration of Independence was adopted on July 4, 1776, and it declared the thirteen colonies free from British rule.
Winston Churchill, known for his leadership during World War II, was also a Nobel Prize-winning writer.
Mahatma Gandhi pioneered the philosophy of nonviolent resistance to lead India to independence from British colonial rule in 1947.
The Berlin Wall stood from 1961 to 1989, physically and ideologically dividing East and West Berlin during the Cold War.
The League of Nations was the first international organization created to maintain world peace, preceding the United Nations.
