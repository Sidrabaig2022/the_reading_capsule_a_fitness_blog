The human brain has about 86 billion neurons, each capable of forming thousands of synaptic connections to other neurons.
Your heart beats around 100,000 times per day, pumping about 2,000 gallons of blood through your body.
The liver performs over 500 functions, including detoxification, protein synthesis, and production of biochemicals necessary for digestion.
Bones are about 5 times stronger than steel of the same density.
The surface area of the human lungs is roughly the same size as a tennis court.
The stomach's acid is strong enough to dissolve metal; it contains hydrochloric acid with a pH of 1.5 to 3.5.
Human skin regenerates itself approximately every 27 days.
Your body produces about 1 to 1.5 quarts of saliva each day.
The human eye can distinguish approximately 10 million different colors.
The average adult human body is made up of around 60% water.
