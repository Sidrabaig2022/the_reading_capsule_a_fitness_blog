The first programmable computer, the Z3, was built by Konrad Zuse in 1941 in Germany.
The internet was originally developed by the U.S. Department of Defense in the 1960s as a communication network called ARPANET.
Moore's Law states that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles about every two years, boosting computing power.
Blockchain technology, the basis for cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, offers secure, decentralized data storage.
3D printing is revolutionizing industries by allowing rapid prototyping and even the creation of human tissue and organs.
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is now used in fields ranging from healthcare diagnostics to autonomous driving systems.
Quantum computing uses quantum bits (qubits) which can represent multiple states simultaneously, offering huge computational power.
The first smartphone, IBM's Simon, was released in 1994 and had features like email, calendar, and touch screen.
Renewable technologies such as solar panels are now more efficient and affordable, transforming global energy usage.
CRISPR technology allows for precise gene editing, potentially curing genetic diseases and improving crop yields.
