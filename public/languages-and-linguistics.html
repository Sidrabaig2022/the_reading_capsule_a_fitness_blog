Languages & Linguistics

‚Üê Back to Knowledge Hub
There are over 7,000 languages spoken around the world today, but about 90% are spoken by fewer than 100,000 people.
The most widely spoken language in the world is English, followed closely by Mandarin Chinese and Hindi.
Sign languages are fully developed languages with their own grammar, vocabulary, and cultural nuances.
The Rosetta Stone was the key to deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphs thanks to its inscriptions in Greek and Demotic.
Esperanto, created in the 19th century, is the most successful constructed international auxiliary language.
The Basque language is unrelated to any other known language and is considered a linguistic isolate.
Tone languages, like Mandarin and Vietnamese, use pitch to distinguish word meaning.
The word "alphabet" comes from the first two Greek letters, alpha and beta.
Some Indigenous Australian languages use cardinal directions (north, south, east, west) instead of left and right.
Children can distinguish between different language sounds from birth, but lose this ability as they specialize in their native tongue.
