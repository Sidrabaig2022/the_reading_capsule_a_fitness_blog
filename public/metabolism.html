What is Metabolism?

Metabolism is a term that‚Äôs often thrown around in wellness spaces, but what does it really mean? üí° Understanding Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Every person has a Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) ‚Äî the number of calories your body needs to function at rest. This BMR is influenced by several factors:

Digestion is the journey your food takes from the moment it enters your mouth to the time your body absorbs the nutrients ‚Äî and finally, eliminates the waste. It‚Äôs a beautifully complex system that works every time you eat, whether it's a full meal or a tiny snack.

ü•Ñ Understanding Digestion: From Bite to Bloodstream

How your body turns food into fuel, love, and glow

We often think of digestion as just ‚Äúeating and pooping‚Äù ‚Äî but oh girl, it‚Äôs *so* much more magical and complex than that. Your digestion is a deeply orchestrated, step-by-step process that transforms every bite of food into the nutrients your cells crave for energy, healing, beauty, and strength. ‚ú®


Let‚Äôs walk through the entire journey of digestion ‚Äî from the very first bite to the moment those nutrients enter your bloodstream and support your metabolism, immunity, brain, and beyond.


üçΩÔ∏è Step 1: It All Starts in Your Mouth (Yes, Really!)

Digestion doesn't begin in your stomach ‚Äî it actually starts the moment you see or smell food. Your brain gets excited and sends signals to your digestive system to get ready.

Then, as you begin to chew, your teeth break the food into smaller pieces while your saliva (which contains an enzyme called amylase) starts breaking down starches. This is why chewing slowly and mindfully is so important ‚Äî you're giving your body a head start.

üí°

Quick tip

Try chewing each bite around 20‚Äì30 times. It may sound like a lot, but it helps digestion and even prevents bloating.

üßÉ Step 2: The Slippery Slide ‚Äî Down the Esophagus

Once the food is chewed into a soft ball called a bolus, it travels down the esophagus ‚Äî the muscular tube that connects your mouth to your stomach. This process is called peristalsis, which is basically a wave-like motion that gently squeezes food downward.

üí°

And nope ‚Äî gravity isn't what moves your food. Even if you eat upside-down (please don't üòÖ), your esophagus still gets the job done.

üß™ Step 3: Welcome to the Stomach ‚Äî Acid Time!

Your stomach is like a bubbling cauldron of acid and enzymes. As the food arrives, it mixes with gastric juices that contain hydrochloric acid (HCl) and pepsin, which break down proteins.

This step is crucial because:

  • It kills harmful bacteria.
  • It turns solid food into a thick liquid called chyme.
  • It begins breaking down protein-rich foods like meat, dairy, tofu, and legumes.

The stomach also has a muscular wall that churns the food like a washing machine, making sure everything is blended well before moving on.

üí°

Beauty bonus

Good stomach acid = better protein absorption = healthier hair, skin, and nails. ‚ú®

üß¨ Step 4: The Small Intestine ‚Äî Where the Magic Happens

Once the chyme is ready, it slowly enters the small intestine, and THIS is where most digestion and nutrient absorption actually happens.

Here's how it works:

  • Your pancreas releases digestive enzymes to break down carbs, proteins, and fats.
  • Your liver produces bile, and your gallbladder stores and releases it to help digest fats.
  • Tiny finger-like projections called villi and microvilli in the intestinal lining absorb nutrients into the bloodstream.
üí°

Fun fact: If you stretched out your small intestine, it would be about 20 feet long! That's a lot of space for nutrient absorption.

What gets absorbed here?

  • Amino acids (from proteins)
  • Simple sugars (from carbs)
  • Fatty acids and glycerol (from fats)
  • Vitamins, minerals, and water

‚ù§Ô∏è Step 5: Into the Bloodstream ‚Äî Fuel for the Body

Now that everything is broken down into its smallest pieces, it enters your bloodstream through the walls of your small intestine. This is where food becomes fuel.

From here:

  • Glucose powers your brain and muscles.
  • Amino acids build and repair tissues.
  • Fatty acids help create hormones and cell membranes.
  • Vitamins and minerals keep everything balanced and functioning.

It's like giving your body little love packages with everything it needs to thrive. üíå

üìÆ

Your liver plays a big role here too ‚Äî it filters the nutrients, detoxifies harmful substances, and decides what to store and what to send out. Kind of like the post office of your digestion.

üöΩ Step 6: The Large Intestine ‚Äî The Final Stop

Whatever your body doesn't absorb moves on to the large intestine (aka the colon). Here:

  • Water is reabsorbed.
  • Electrolytes like sodium and potassium are balanced.
  • Fiber is fermented by gut bacteria to create healthy fatty acids.
  • Waste is formed into stool and prepared for elimination.

This step might not seem glamorous, but it's vital. A happy colon = regular poops, less bloating, clearer skin, and a strong immune system. üåø

üí°

Gut tip

Eat enough fiber, drink water, and support your gut microbiome with probiotics for a smoother ride.

üåü Why This Matters for Your Metabolism

If your digestion isn't functioning properly, your metabolism doesn't get the nutrients it needs to keep your energy high and your body in balance. You could be eating super healthy ‚Äî but if your gut isn't absorbing those nutrients well, you won't feel the benefits.

So if you want a faster metabolism, glowing skin, better focus, and balanced hormones ‚Äî you have to support your digestion first.
